Research

My research focuses on the ecological, evolutionary, and behavioral drivers of respiratory virus dynamics, with a particular emphasis on seasonal influenza, RSV, and SARS-CoV-2.

Current Research

  • Linking human mobility to respiratory virus transmission dynamics — How population movement patterns shape the spread of respiratory pathogens

  • Influenza ecology and evolution — The role of antigenic drift and subtype interference in seasonal flu epidemics

  • Disease forecasting — Operational forecasts and scenario projections for respiratory virus outbreaks

Past Research

  • PhD: Gut microbiomes in wild mammals — How social networks and proximity to other host species shape gut microbial communities in Malagasy lemurs
